Understanding Mechanical Switches

Cherry MX Red (Linear)

Smooth, fast, no tactile bump. Best for gaming where speed matters. Quieter than clicky switches. Linear feel throughout keypress.

Cherry MX Brown (Tactile)

Slight bump on keypress. Good balance between gaming and typing. Quieter than Blue switches. The "Goldilocks" option for most users.

Cherry MX Blue (Clicky)

Audible click with tactile bump. Satisfying for typing. Loud—not suitable for offices or shared spaces. Beloved by writers who love feedback.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Are mechanical keyboards worth the cost?

A: Absolutely if you type 4+ hours daily. Better ergonomics, faster typing, improved accuracy. Cost is offset by increased productivity and reduced fatigue.

Q: Will my roommate hate me for the noise?

A: Depends on switch type. Red/Brown switches are quieter. Blue switches are notoriously loud. Use o-rings to dampen sound.

Q: Can I replace switches?

A: Yes if hot-swappable. Most modern keyboards offer this. Some soldered keyboards require desoldering—requires skill.

Q: Do I need RGB lighting?

A: No. It's a nice aesthetic but doesn't improve typing. Adds cost and power consumption. Leopold keyboards skip RGB entirely.

Q: What's the learning curve?

A: Minimal. Most people adjust in 2-3 days. The improved typing feel makes the transition quick and enjoyable.
